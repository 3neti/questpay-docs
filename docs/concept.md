## Game Premise

QuestPay‚Ñ¢ contestants (individuals or teams) embark on a multi-stage adventure where progression requires completing verified digital payment transactions.

### The Core Loop

1. **Receive a QuestPay voucher code** triggered by an action (completing previous challenge, registration, CTA click, etc.) and delivered via SMS or email with optional QR representation
2. **Complete the mission** involving a real-world transaction or task
3. **Redeem the voucher** which triggers x-Change's rich redemption flow:
   - Splash page displays challenge message and instructions
   - **Redemption wizard collects required inputs and proof** (text responses, receipt photos, location check-ins, signatures, KYC data, survey responses)
   - Contestant must complete all required inputs to redeem successfully
   - Upon successful redemption, contestant redirected to interactive landing page
4. **Game operator receives real-time feedback** via webhook with:
   - Redemption status and collected data
   - Automatic verification via x-Change webhooks and API validation
   - Completion status
5. **Landing page presents interactive challenge elements:**
   - Challenge details and mission objectives
   - **Sponsor activations**: Download mobile app, register for service, watch video/ad
   - **Interactive tasks**: Solve puzzles, complete captcha, answer trivia
   - **Educational content**: Tutorial videos, product demos, brand storytelling
   - Links to merchants, locations, or partner platforms
   - **Next voucher code revealed** after verification is complete
6. **Progress toward final quest** and grand prize (return to step 1 with new voucher)

### What Makes It Work

- **Real transactions** = Real verification (no faking it)
- **Digital trail** = Transparent, auditable gameplay
- **Partner integration** = Merchants and services become game locations
- **Scalable difficulty** = From simple purchases to complex multi-step missions
- **Viewer participation** = Audience can follow along and predict outcomes

## Gameplay Variations

### Individual vs. Team

**Individual Play**:

- Solo contestants compete for personal glory
- Emphasis on speed and strategy
- Simpler logistics, easier to film

**Team Play**:

- 2-5 person teams with assigned roles
- Requires coordination and communication
- More complex challenges possible
- Better for corporate or friend/family editions

### Competitive vs. Cooperative

**Competitive Mode**:

- Race format - first to complete wins
- Head-to-head challenges
- Leaderboards and eliminations
- Higher drama and tension

**Cooperative Mode**:

- All players work toward shared goal
- Unlocking group rewards
- Community vs. challenge
- More feel-good, less cutthroat

## Sample Episode Arc

### Episode Structure (1 hour broadcast)

**Act 1: Setup (15 min)**

- Recap of previous episode
- Introduction of today's theme/challenge zone
- Contestants receive their first QuestPay code
- Strategy discussions and predictions

**Act 2: The Quest (30 min)**

- Contestants navigate to locations
- Complete payment-based challenges
- Real-time verification and progression
- Drama, setbacks, and victories
- Social media integration showing viewer reactions

**Act 3: Showdown (15 min)**

- Final challenge requiring all previous codes
- Live leaderboard updates
- Winner announcement
- Preview of next episode's theme
- Distribution of next starting codes

## Progression Design

### Season Structure

**Pilot (Season 0)**:

- 3-5 episodes
- Proof of concept
- Local/regional scope
- Limited contestants (10-15)

**Full Season**:

- 10-12 episodes
- National coverage
- 20-50 contestants
- Weekly eliminations or point accumulation

**Franchise Edition**:

- Multiple simultaneous seasons in different cities/countries
- Cross-regional championships
- Celebrity or VIP special editions

## Challenge Design Principles

### Every Challenge Must Be:

1. **Verifiable** - Digital transaction proof required
2. **Fair** - Equal opportunity regardless of location/resources
3. **Legal** - Compliant with local regulations
4. **Safe** - No dangerous or unethical requirements
5. **Engaging** - Interesting to watch and participate in
6. **Educational** - Teaches something about digital payments
7. **Sponsor-friendly** - Natural brand integration opportunities

### Challenge Difficulty Curve

| Stage | Difficulty | Transaction Type | Goal |
|-------|-----------|------------------|------|
| Early Episodes | Easy | Simple purchases, donations | Build confidence, teach mechanics |
| Mid-Season | Moderate | Multi-step transactions, budgeting | Test strategy and resourcefulness |
| Finals | Hard | Complex chains, time pressure | Reward skill and preparation |

## Audience Engagement

### Interactive Elements

- **Prediction Games**: Viewers guess winners, earn rewards
- **Side Quests**: Audience members complete parallel challenges
- **Social Voting**: Influence challenge types or contestant advantages
- **Live Commentary**: Real-time discussion on social platforms
- **Challenge Yourself Mode**: Try the same challenges at home

### Second Screen Experience

- Mobile app showing live maps
- Real-time transaction feeds (anonymized)
- Contestant stats and histories
- Sponsored mini-games and offers
